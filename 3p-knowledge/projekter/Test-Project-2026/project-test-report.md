## Resumé
This document outlines the NH3 / ATEX project for Unibio A/S at Kalundborg, focusing on remediation of installation deviations, certificate verification, lightning protection upgrade, and full commissioning preparation according to DS/EN 60079-14:2024 and DS/EN 62305-3. Critical safety issues found during præinspektion include insufficient lightning protection bonding and damaged EX d cable glands.

## Baggrund
Customer: Unibio A/S, Location: Asnæsvej 2A, 4400 Kalundborg, Date: 13 March 2026

## Standarder og Krav
- DS/EN 60079-14:2024
- DS/EN 62305-3
- ATEX directives (2014/34/EU)

## Best Practices
- All EX equipment must carry valid certificates (IECEx or ATEX)
- Cable glands must be EX-certified and correctly torqued to IP rating
- Junction boxes in Zone 1 must be EX d or EX e rated and fully closed
- PE conductor cross-section ≥50mm² for lightning protection bonding
- All instrument cables in Zone 1: shielded, min 0.75mm²

## Kendte Problemer og Afvigelser
- Lightning protection bonding insufficient (6mm² vs 50mm² required) — critical safety issue
- Damaged EX d cable glands may allow gas ingress into EX instruments in Zone 1
- Missing equipment certificates for key instruments may delay commissioning approval

## Tjekliste
### Requirements
- Verify certificates for all EX equipment
- Replace damaged EX d cable glands on all NH3 instruments in Zone 1
- Tighten and re-certify junction box cover on 80HLS002
- Upgrade lightning protection bonding conductor to 50mm²
- Install missing PE conductor on NH3 panel
- Obtain and file EX certificates for 80PT004 and 80HLS002
- Commission full lightning protection survey (DS/EN 62305-3)
- Set up periodic inspection schedule per DS/EN 60079-17
### Cable Specifications
- Instrument cables in Zone 1: shielded, min 0.75mm²

## Lessons Learned
- Critical safety issues must be addressed before commissioning
- Regular inspection and maintenance are essential for ATEX installations
- Compliance with relevant standards is crucial for ensuring safety
