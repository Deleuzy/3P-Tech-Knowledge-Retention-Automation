## Lessons Learned

**ATEX Motor Installation - Building B**

- **Root Cause**: The original motor specification (Ex d - explosion proof) was changed late in the project to Ex e (increased safety) due to a misinterpretation of the zone classification. This required re-wiring of the entire motor circuit, additional certification documentation, and re-inspection by the ATEX auditor. The change caused 2 weeks delay and required emergency ordering of compatible cables and glands. Total financial impact: 75,000 DKK in additional material costs and 40 hours of rework (32,000 DKK labor).
- **What Happened**: 1. Always verify zone classification with the ATEX coordinator BEFORE finalizing equipment specifications. 2. Get a second review of all Zone 1/2 equipment selections by a qualified ATEX consultant. 3. Include ATEX certification costs and potential change costs in initial budget estimates. 4. Document any zone classification assumptions in the early project phases.
