# COMPLIANCE ANALYSIS REPORT
## Document: Yara Gas Telemetric System - Yara Gas (Electrical Schematic)
**Standard Applied:** [STD1] installation-trace-heating-xxxxxxxx (Trace Heating Installation Standard)

---

## FINDINGS

### 1. **Standard Inapplicability — CRITICAL**
The document is an electrical schematic for a gas telemetric monitoring system (Yara Gas Level Detection) with programmable logic controllers, analog outputs, and data acquisition infrastructure. [STD1] governs trace heating cable installation on piping systems only. This schematic does not depict trace heating cable design, routing, insulation, or thermal management.

**Action:** This document falls outside [STD1]'s scope. No compliance assessment under [STD1] is valid. The schematic should be evaluated against electrical control system standards (e.g., IEC 61131, EN 61326 for control/instrumentation equipment).

---

### 2. **No Trace Heating Cable Components Visible — OK**
The schematic shows process value outputs (Yara_Fuse_OK, IL200.5, J2304.3, K1041), sensor connections (W_Yara-1, shielded 2×0.75 conductors), and control junction boxes (Q2210, Q2215), but does not depict any trace heating cable or thermal management elements that would require compliance with [STD1 §2–5].

**Action:** No trace heating-specific installation checks required.

---

### 3. **Shielded Cable Conductor Grounding Not Addressed — WARNING**
The schematic labels a shielded conductor pair (2×0.75 mm, "W_Yara-3 Shielded") but does not explicitly show ground termination at either end. [STD1 §7.3] requires the braided shield or ground conductor to be connected to the installation's ground.

**Action:** Verify that the shield on all shielded instrumentation cables is grounded at the control panel and at remote sensor/junction box locations. Add ground termination detail to schematic or provide separate grounding diagram.

---

### 4. **Residual Current Device (RCD) Protection Not Shown — WARNING**
The schematic displays 24V DC and signal circuits but does not indicate an RCD (maximum 30 mA rating) at the power supply or distribution point. [STD1 §7.2] mandates RCD protection to minimize electric shock and fire risk.

**Action:** Confirm that the 24V power supply circuit (originating from off-schematic 24V_1 source) is protected by a 30 mA RCD. If not, add RCD to main power distribution panel and label on updated schematic.

---

### 5. **Electrical Installation by Qualified Personnel Not Documented — WARNING**
The schematic provides design intent but does not reference installation execution by authorized electrician personnel. [STD1 §7.1] requires all electrical materials to be installed by an authorized electrician.

**Action:** Attach installation checklist or certification requirement confirming that field installation of this system is performed by a licensed electrician. Document sign-off on as-built documentation.

---

## SUMMARY

**Applicable Clauses:** [STD1] does not apply to gas telemetry/instrumentation schematics; limited applicability to grounding and electrical safety of interconnected circuits only.

**Total Findings:** 5 (1 CRITICAL, 3 WARNING, 1 OK)

**Severity Assessment:**
- **CRITICAL (1):** Fundamental scope mismatch — document is outside [STD1] domain.
- **WARNING (3):** Electrical safety practices (grounding, RCD, personnel qualification) should be verified but are not inherent to trace heating design.

**Recommendation:** Request evaluation against relevant electrical control/instrumentation standards (IEC 61131-2, EN 60204-1, or equivalent) rather than trace heating installation standard. Apply [STD1] only if trace heating cable is subsequently added to process piping.
