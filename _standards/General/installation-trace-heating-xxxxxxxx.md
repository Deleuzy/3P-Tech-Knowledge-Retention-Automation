# Trace Heating Installation Standard

**Domain:** Thermal Management Systems  
**Source:** Heatcom Corporation A/S, Barmstedt Allé 6, 5500 Middelfart, Danmark  
**Language:** Danish (DK)  
**Version:** V1-10/2024

## Scope

This standard establishes requirements for the safe design, preparation, and installation of trace heating cable systems on piping applications, including specification gathering, cable routing, electrical safety, and thermal insulation methods to prevent heat loss and ensure optimal system performance.

---

## Section 1 — Preparation and Design

### Clause 1.1 — Information requirements for dimensioning
The installer shall collect and document the following parameters prior to system dimensioning and installation:
- pipe line length and diameter
- quantity of valves, flanges, and measurement devices
- pipe supports and auxiliary installation equipment

### Clause 1.2 — Critical factors for cable sizing
The installer shall recognize that the parameters specified in Clause 1.1 are determining factors for correct heat cable dimensioning.

---

## Section 2 — Cable Routing and Mechanical Installation

### Clause 2.1 — Alignment with designed supports
The installer shall ensure that the trace heating cable follows the designed pipe supports and components correctly during installation.

### Clause 2.2 — Tension and stress avoidance
The installer shall avoid applying excessive pull or tension to the trace heating cable during installation, as this may damage the cable or cause system failure.

### Clause 2.3 — Cable proximity to pipe
The installer shall maintain the cable in close contact with the pipe, especially when crossing obstacles such as pipe supports or intersecting pipes.

### Clause 2.4 — Correction of misrouted cable
Where the trace heating cable crosses an obstacle incorrectly, the installer may be required to remove and reinstall the cable, or in certain cases, cut and splice the cable.

### Clause 2.5 — Fastening interval for fiberglass tape
The installer shall secure the trace heating cable using fiberglass tape wraps at intervals not exceeding 0.3 meters along the pipe.

### Clause 2.6 — Temperature rating of fastening materials
The installer shall verify that the maximum exposure temperature for aluminum tape and fiberglass tape remains within the application's operating temperature range.

---

## Section 3 — Non-Metallic Pipe Applications

### Clause 3.1 — Barrier requirement for non-metallic pipes
The installer shall cover non-metallic pipes (such as PVC or FRP) with aluminum foil before applying the trace heating cable to prevent direct contact between cable and pipe.

---

## Section 4 — Electrical Connections and Termination

### Clause 4.1 — Correct connection completion
The installer shall ensure that all electrical connections are completed correctly and isolated to prevent short circuits and overheating.

### Clause 4.2 — Termination kit placement
The installer shall mount end and connection kits over the pipe where practical.

### Clause 4.3 — Junction box drainage
The installer shall install drain openings at the bottom of coupling boxes to prevent condensation accumulation.

### Clause 4.4 — Above-ground installation of connections
The installer shall mount all trace heating cable connections above ground level.

---

## Section 5 — Thermal Insulation

### Clause 5.1 — Insulation material thermal properties
The installer shall use thermal insulation materials with properties that exceed the maximum operating temperature of the application.

### Clause 5.2 — Aluminum foil wrapping prior to insulation
The installer shall cover the trace heating cable with aluminum foil after installation on the pipe and before applying thermal insulation to ensure optimal heat transfer between cable and pipe.

### Clause 5.3 — Insulation integrity
After installation of the trace heating cable, the installer shall ensure that the piping is correctly insulated to prevent heat loss.

---

## Section 6 — Multiple Parallel Circuits

### Clause 6.1 — Design cases requiring multiple circuits
The installer shall recognize that two or more parallel circuits of trace heating cable on a pipe are required in the following situations:
- critical processes that require redundant heat circuits, where each circuit shall be sized to deliver sufficient heat independently
- large pipe diameters where a single trace heating cable is insufficient to maintain the required temperature, requiring multiple circuits to ensure uniform and adequate heat transfer

---

## Section 7 — Safety and Compliance Requirements

### Clause 7.1 — Installation by qualified personnel
All electrical materials shall be installed by an authorized electrician.

### Clause 7.2 — Residual current device protection
The installer shall protect the installation with a residual current device (RCD) with a maximum rating of 30 mA to minimize the risk of electric shock and fire.

### Clause 7.3 — Grounding connection
The installer shall connect the trace heating cable's ground conductor or braided shield to the installation's ground.

### Clause 7.4 — Approved connection kits
The installer shall not use connection kits or other electrical materials that are not suitable for this application type.

### Clause 7.5 — Prohibited fastening materials
The installer shall not use vinyl tape or other adhesive tape containing plasticizers to secure the trace heating cable.

### Clause 7.6 — Damaged cable installation prohibition
The installer shall not install cables with visible damage to the outer sheath, as this may cause electric shock.

### Clause 7.7 — Cable repair method
The installer shall not repair sections of damaged cable; instead, the installer shall use splice kits to connect new cable sections as replacements.

### Clause 7.8 — Functional safety requirement
Trace heating cables shall be installed correctly in accordance with this installation standard to ensure safe and adequate function and to prevent electric shock and fire.
